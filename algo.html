Local Intelligence

How YTMusic+ Works (Your Algorithm)

Your algorithm is a Deterministic Content-Based Engine. It does not care what the rest of the world is listening to. It is a pure, mathematical reflection of your personal listening history, built specifically to prevent listening fatigue.

Every time you generate a playlist, the SQL engine (generate_smart_shuffle) executes a highly tuned workflow:

45%

🔥 The Core (High Tier)

We reserve 45% of the playlist for your absolute favorites (Affinity Score > 0.6). These are tracks you obsess over.

35%

⭐ The Orbit (Mid Tier)

We fill 35% with tracks you like and listen to regularly, but aren't your top tracks. This ensures familiarity without burning out your favorites.

20%

🎲 The Fringe (Low Tier)

The final 20% are deep cuts. Tracks you've listened to maybe once or twice. This is where discovery happens.

The Vibe Matrix: If you select a genre (e.g., Techno), it applies a wildcard filter across your entire database first, and then applies the 45/35/20 split to just that subset of music.

Global Intelligence

How Big Tech Works (Spotify / YouTube Music)

Platforms like Spotify and YouTube Music use massive, server-farm-level AI models. They primarily rely on Collaborative Filtering and Deep Reinforcement Learning.

  • Collaborative Filtering: "Users who listened to Track A also listened to Track B." They map billions of users on a giant mathematical grid to find people with taste identical to yours.
  • Audio Embeddings: They analyze the actual audio waveforms using neural networks to detect BPM, acousticness, "danceability", and vocal tone, grouping similar sounding songs together.
  • Contextual RL: The algorithm learns in real-time. If they play a new song and you skip it in 5 seconds, it heavily penalizes that recommendation. If you listen all the way through, it rewards the model.
  • Time & Location: They use metadata like time of day, day of the week, and whether you are on a mobile network vs wifi to guess your current context (e.g., Gym vs Study).
